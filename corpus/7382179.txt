rowing scientific data and literature focusing on the role of food processing secondary and by&#x2010;products in relation to human well&#x2010;being. Coincidently, there is an increase in the consumers' information regarding noncommunicable diet&#x2010;associated diseases (Chernukhaa &amp; Fedulova, 2015). Thus, the demand for nonchemical, natural, safe, and health&#x2010;improving food components is also growing (Schieber, Stintzing, &amp; Carle, 2001). Large quantities of agricultural secondary or by&#x2010;products generated after food processing have become the main issue concerning food industry worldwide, since they could lead to environmental including pollution (soil, water, air). Furthermore, the disposal of agricultural by&#x2010;products could cost huge amount to treat under certain governmental regulations (Gowe,&#xA0;2015). Additionally, it is no longer pragmatic to discard by&#x2010;products therefore; utilization of by&#x2010;products has become an alternative method to overcome this issue (Lafarga &amp; Hayes,&#xA0;2014; Zhao, Chen, &amp; Du,&#xA0;2012). Particularly, when food processing by&#x2010;products have considerable quantity of valuable and favorable raw bioactive functional compounds, therefore they can be useful for both technological and pharmaceutical purposes (Azyyati &amp; Yen, 2014; Schieber et al., 2001).
      For recovery of biologically active compound in food by&#x2010;products, different conventional methods including solvent&#x2010;based extraction have been used. However, due to more demand for eco&#x2010;friendly, cheap, and high&#x2010;efficiency methods, novel techniques are superior to conventional methods (Gen&#xE7;da&#x11F;, G&#xF6;rg&#xFC;&#xE7;, &amp; Y&#x131;lmaz,&#xA0;2020) and novel methods have been studied and examined. The methods used include membrane&#x2010;based technologies including microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration (Castro&#x2010;Mu&#xF1;oz, Conidi, &amp; Cassano,&#xA0;2019), ultrasound&#x2010;assisted&#xA0;extraction (Sabater, Sabater, Olano, Montilla, &amp; Corzo,&#xA0;2020), microwave&#x2010;assisted extraction (Casazza, Pettinato, &amp; Perego,&#xA0;2020), electrotechnologies, ultrasound, high hydrostatic pressure, nanotechnology, and pressurized fluid (Zhu et al., 2020). The aim of this review was to present and give an overview about the most common biologically active compound present in a wide range of food products. Furthermore, the review also presents the by&#x2010;product bioactive compound in different food products such as milk, eggs, meat, cereals, fruit and vegetable, herbs and spices, coffee and tea, honey and sugar, and additives. Moreover, health and pharmaceutical benefits of the bioactive compounds will be presented (Table 1).

      METHODOLOGY
      This systematic review was done through collecting data from databases such as PubMed and Web of Science as well as articles available in Google Scholar. The exclusion area included articles published before 1980, non&#x2010;peer&#x2010;reviewed articles, articles published in other languages than English, and nonoriginal articles including letters, editorial conference summaries, and paper without abstract. Therefore, only articles met these requirements were accepted.

      FOOD PROCESSING BY&#x2010;PRODUCTS

        3.1
        Fruits
        Fruits are one of the food materials that undergo industrial processing which could lead to various waste materials in different forms and shapes during pre&#x2010; and postharvesting process in growing, preparation, and processing (Joshi &amp; Devrajan,&#xA0;2007). As a result of that diversity, the by&#x2010;product left is extremely dissimilar because of the difference in the fruits various industrial processes and producing different products. Some fruits result in 25%&#x2013;30% by&#x2010;product or nonedible waste products (Ajila et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2009). However, less waste agribusiness has become a global need nowadays to overcome waste problem.
        According to the fruit waste part; peel, seed, and stones produced after fruit processing, they could be importantly utilized as sources of many bioactive components for agroindustry to role in the aspect and transform these by&#x2010;products into valuable products (Tuchila, Jianu, Rujescu, &amp; Butur,&#xA0;2008). In a study about bioactive compounds, it was found that the mango peel and seed contained the highest quantity of bioactive compound. The peel included 5.997&#xA0;mg of gallic acid/g of fresh weight (fw), 4.455&#xA0;mg of quercetin/g fw, and 47.97% DPPH&#x2010;free radical scavenging activity at the concentration of 322&#xA0;mg/ml, while the seed presented 37.279&#xA0;mg of gallic acid/g, 35.954&#xA0;mg of quercetin/g on fw basis, and 93.4% of DPPH&#x2010;free radical scavenging activity at the concentration of 307&#xA0;mg/ml (Ayala&#x2010;Zavala, Rosas&#x2010;Dom&#xED;nguez, Vega&#x2010;Vega, &amp; Gonz&#xE1;lez&#x2010;Aguilar,&#xA0;2010). In another study regarding the phenolic and antioxidant in some fruits including mango, longan, jackfruit, and avocado, following different analytical method ABTS (2,2&#x2010;azinobis&#x2010;3&#x2010;ethylbenzothiazoline&#x2010;6&#x2010;sulfonic acid), FRAP (ferric&#x2010;reducing antioxidant power), and FCR (Folin&#x2013;Ciocalteu reagent) have been used. The research found that the seeds of these fruits contained more antioxidant capacity and phenolic content than the pulp. The ABTS, FRAP, and FCR values for the seeds of mango, longan, avocado, and jackfruit were as follows: 762, 448, 236.1, and 7.4&#xA0;&#x3BC;mol of ascorbic acid/g; 2,572, 1,388, 1,484, and 2.8&#xA0;&#x3BC;mol of gallic acid equivalents/g; and 117, 62.6, 88.6, and 27.2&#xA0;mg of gallic acid equivalents/g, respectively. The ABTS, FRAP, and FCR values for the pulp of mango, longan, avocado, and jackfruit were as follows: 7.2, 3.7, 4.9, and 3.0&#xA0;&#x3BC;mol of life&#x2010;protecting phytochemicals and antioxidants (Galali, Aziz, &amp; Ali,&#xA0;2017). Furthermore, depending on the type of the fruit and part of the fruit, high percentage of the antioxidants can be obtained such as vitamins C and E, phenolic compounds including phenyl&#x2010;propanoids and flavonoids, and/or carotenoids such as lycopene. Similar to the entire tissue, by&#x2010;products are rich in phytochemical, antioxidant, and antimicrobial compounds than the end products (Ayala&#x2010;Zavala et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2010). Terpenes or terpenoids are powerful against microbes. The peel and seed of avocado exhibited antimicrobial activity against some gram&#x2010;negative and gram&#x2010;positive bacteria and yeast. It was also observed that the seed and peel extract of &#x201C;Hass&#x201D; avocado showed a minimum inhibitory activity with 104.2&#xA0;&#x3BC;g/ml against Salmonella enteriditis and Zygosaccharomyces bailii (Raymond Chia &amp; Dykes,&#xA0;2010). Lemon extracts were used in dairy products and improved the shelf life of mozzarella cheese. The skin contained hydrolyzable tannins and flavonoids, including anthocyanin 3,5&#x2010;diglucosides, quercetin, myricetin, and kaempferol glycosides ( Sandhu &amp; Gu,&#xA0;2010). It has been found that the most common phenol compounds in grape seeds were seemed to be flavan&#x2010;3&#x2010;ols; most of them are gallocatechin gallate and catechin. The skins were mostly flavonols, that is, quercetin and myricetin. Determination of anthocyanins in the berry skin by ultra&#x2010;high&#x2010;performance liquid chromatography discovered twenty derivatives of malvidin, delphinidin, petunidin, cyanidin, and peonidin (Panteli&#x107; et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2016). Fruits by&#x2010;products are also important for antimicrobial activity against some pathogenic, spoilage bacteria, and yeasts. The most abundant studied antimicrobials are essential oils. They composed of a combination of 5&#x2010;carbon and controlled rancidity during storage. Antibacterial activity of grapes extracts was studied. They showed positive activity against some pathogenic bacteria. Therefore, fruit by&#x2010;products are promising source of antimicrobial agents (Amaral, Ekins, Richards, &amp; Knowles, 1998).
        Fruit by&#x2010;products are also a good source of antioxidant that can be used as antibrowning agent that could happen to free cut fruits and reduced the quality of the products. It has been reported that low percentage of ascorbic acid reduced the browning reaction in fresh&#x2010;cut peaches and apple slices and fresh&#x2010;cut pineapple. Also, extracts from Palo Fierro rich in antioxidants reduced the browning of apple juice.
        Fruit by&#x2010;products are a good source of natural colorant and pigments due to high stability, purity, availability, and low cost particularly when the synthetic colorants are publically rejected due to health concerns. One of the common colorants that is extracted from different fruit by&#x2010;products is grape pomace (Stintzing &amp; Carle,&#xA0;2004) blueberry(Bobinait&#x117; et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2016) and some other guajiru, jambolao, jussara, and acai (Sousa De Brito et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2007).
        Fruit by&#x2010;products are significantly rich in bioactive dietary fibers which are crucial in prevention in many diet&#x2010;associated diseases (Zhu, Du, Zheng, &amp; Li,&#xA0;2015). Dietary fiber and fiber&#x2010;rich by&#x2010;products of food processing present high technical and pharmaceutical properties. Thus, they have been supplemented to many food products (Elleuch, Bedigian, Roiseux, &amp; Besbes, 2011). In a study about the percentage of neutral dietary fiber, acid dietary fiber cellulose, hemicelluloses, and lignin based on 100&#xA0;g dry matter, it was found that the percentages of dietary fiber were up to 63% (Table&#xA0;2; Verma &amp; Joshi, 2000).

        Protein is also another component of the fruit by&#x2010;products particularly seed and kernel flour which can be an inexpensive source to be exploited for different purposes.         3.2
        Vegetables
        Vegetable by&#x2010;products composed of different parts: peels, seeds, stones, and leaves. They could be source of different materials such as antioxidants such as vitamins C and E, phenolic compounds including phenyl&#x2010;propanoids and flavonoids, and/or carotenoids such as lycopene can be found (Ayala&#x2010;Zavala et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2010).
        Vegetable by&#x2010;products are important sources of phenolic compounds that present and can be extracted using different solvents. Various by&#x2010;products seemed to contain different bioactive compounds. Asparagus waste seems to have the highest phenolic compounds, and tomato showed the lowest phenolic compounds (Table&#xA0;3; Peschel et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2006). It was found that using acetone solvent could be very powerful to extract phenolic compounds. It has been reported that caffeic acid derivatives are the major component in artichoke by&#x2010;products with a prevalent range of caffeoylquinic acid derivatives with chlorogenic acid (5&#x2010;O&#x2010;caffeoylquinic acid) as the most important of these derivatives. Some other phenolic compounds such as the flavonoids apigenin and luteolin (both glucosides and rutinosides) as well as different cyanidin caffeoylglucoside derivatives were determined (Llorach, Esp&#xED;n, Tom&#xE1;s&#x2010;Barber&#xE1;n, &amp; Ferreres,&#xA0;2002). Furthermore, by&#x2010;product from lettuce showed the presence of hydroxycinnamic acids and flavonoids. The flavonoids made of flavones (luteolin derivatives) and flavonols (quercetin derivatives), whereas by&#x2010;products from chicory made of only of kaempferol derivatives (Llorach, Tom&#xE1;s&#x2010;Barber&#xE1;n, &amp; Ferreres,&#xA0;2004). Cauliflower by&#x2010;products also showed to contain phenolic compounds. It has been stated that cauliflower by&#x2010;products contain flavonoids and hydroxycinnamic acids (caffeic acid and sinapic acid). Flavonols such as kaempferol and quercetin with sinapic acid and glucose seemed to be the major phenolics available (Llorach, Esp&#xED;n, Tom&#xE1;s&#x2010;Barber&#xE1;n, &amp; Ferreres,&#xA0;2003).

In addition, cellulose in pea vines and hemicellulose in baby corn husk were found with highest percentage by 36.8 and 32.1, respectively. As it is the source of table sugar, sugar beet leaves showed the highest percentage of sugar by 24.9. Regarding specific proteins, albumin in cauliflower leaves, globulin in cabbage leaves, prolamin in black chickpea plant, and glutelin in pea vines were found with 62.4, 16.2, 27.6, and 22.7, respectively. Finally, percentage of the phenolic content of the radish leaves seemed to be the highest by 6.9% comparing to others (Table 7; Wadhwa &amp; Bakshi,&#xA0;2013). The above data show that the vegetable by&#x2010;products are rich in bioactive compounds and can be useful pharmaceutically.

        A number of studies have tried to use the coffee by&#x2010;product in different aspects for new products. Some researchers endeavored to produce bioethanol from the coffee by&#x2010;product. A combination of enzymatic treatment, heating, and steam was used. The results showed that ethanol efficiency of 83% per amount of glucose and yield bioethanol ranged from 0.426&#xA0;&#xB1;&#xA0;0.0015&#xA0;g/L (Arrizon et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2012). Another study used high pressure and sulfuric acid to hydrolyze dried coffee by&#x2010;products. The results showed that ethanol yield of 82&#xA0;g/kg dry coffee pulp and the amount of ethanol produced was 0.45&#xA0;g/g sugar (Shenoy et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2011). Gouvea et al. (2009) optimized best condition for ethanol production; it was found that using Saccharomyces Cerevisiae in sticky coffee husk fermentation resulted in best yield condition with the optimal temperature at 30&#xB0;C and 3&#xA0;g yeast/L. The yielded bioethanol was 8.49&#xA0;&#xB1;&#xA0;0.29&#xA0;g/100&#xA0;g dry husk (13.57&#xA0;&#xB1;&#xA0;0.45&#xA0;g ethanol/L).
        For health benefits, coffee by&#x2010;products can be useful due to its content of bioactive compounds. Various studies have confirmed the fact that coffee by&#x2010;products could be useful for health purposes. In a study about the benefits of coffee by&#x2010;products in relation to prebiotic, antimicrobial, and antioxidant characteristics, it has been concluded that coffee silver skin and coffee spent grounds can potentially be used as functional ingredients. Furthermore, both of them can be useful as a source of prebiotic compounds, but melanoidins should be removed. Coffee silver skin and coffee spent grounds could be utilized as natural preservatives if used in large amount. Moreover, coffee silver skin and coffee spent grounds can be an important component to improve human health because of its antioxidant activity (Jim&#xE9;nez&#x2010;Zamora,&#xA0;2015). In another study, the antimicrobial activity of biologically active in coffee waste was studied, and it was found that coffee by&#x2010;products showed an inhibitory activity against S.&#xA0;aureus and Escherichia &#xA0;coli. A stronger inhibition was also observed against Candida sp. growth (C.&#xA0;albicans, C.&#xA0;Krusei, and C.&#xA0;parapsilosis [Sousa, Gabriel, Cerqueira, Manso, &amp; Vinha,&#xA0;2015]).

        3.4
        Tea
        Tea is one of the most consumed drinks worldwide and possesses strong phenolic content (Vladi&#x107; et al., 2016). One of the waste parts is stalk and stem which are rich in dietary fiber removed during tea processing. However, tea leave waste is the main by&#x2010;products of tea leaf industry. Tea by&#x2010;products are normally disposed of as compost, dumped into lands, and/or burned. But these are not a reasonable solution since they cause both environmental and economic problems (Hossain, Ko, &amp; Yang, 2012).
        It has been reported that the flavanols are the most important compound of tea polyphenols that predominantly include catechins, such as epicatechin, epicatechin gallate, epigallocatechin, epigallocatechin gallate, and catechin. Since the tea leftover could contain similar components but with different quantities, a study was done on phenolic content of the black tea waste sample, oven waste, and grade waste. The study concluded that tea waste is an important source of antioxidant and antimicrobial activity that can be used for different purposes such as food, pharmaceutical, cosmetic, and agricultural sectors. It was also stated that aqueous ethanol solvents could be the cheapest, nontoxic green alternative for antioxidant and antimicrobial phenolics from tea waste.

        3.7
        Nuts
        Nut by&#x2010;products could include skin or testa, hard shell, green leafy cover, hull, and leaf. These are important and valuable sources of bioactive compounds that have multifunctional traits and antioxidant activity, and antimutagenic, anticarcinogenic, and antiproliferative properties (Shahidi &amp; Ambigaipalan,&#xA0;2015). The bioactive compounds of these components involve in the protection of the body directly or indirectly through detrimental free radicals and diminish the risks of the diet&#x2010;associated diseases. Thus, inclusion of these compounds in the daily meal is highly recommended by health expertise which can protect the body from harmful compounds (Alasalvar &amp; Bolling,&#xA0;2015). Regarding the nut coproducts, there are different biologically active compounds that have been determined and reported. Flavonoids including catechin, epicatechin, eriodictyol&#x2010;7&#x2010;O&#x2010;glucoside, quercetin&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;rutinoside, quercetin&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;galactoside, quercetin&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;glucoside, kaempferol&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;rutinoside, naringenin&#x2010;7&#x2010;O&#x2010;glucoside, isorhamnetin&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;rutinoside, kaempferol&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;glucoside, isorhamnetin&#x2010;3&#x2010;O&#x2010;glucoside, eriodictyol, quercetin, naringenin, kaempferol, and isorhamnetin are commonly found in the coproducts of almond, hazelnut, and pistachio. Recently, procyanidin dimers, trimers, and tetramers as well as dihydrochalcones such as phloretin&#x2010;2&#x2010;O&#x2010;glucoside have been reported only in hazelnut pellet. Isoflavones, such as daidzein and genistein, have not been found in pistachio coproducts compared with raw pistachio. Flavones, such as luteolin and 5, 7&#x2010;dihydroxychromone, were only found in peanut shell. In addition, apigenin is only determined in pistachio hard shell, while diosmetin is found only in peanut skin (Table&#xA0;17). So far, no studies have been reported about the flavonoid contents of the shell and hull/ pellet of almond, peanut, and Brazil nut. In this connection, the flavonoids of cashew coproducts have not been reported here (Chang, Alasalvar, Bolling, &amp; Shahidi, 2016).

        Egg
        Egg by&#x2010;products mainly include eggshells and membranes (King'ori, 2017), wastewater from egg processing industry (Xu, Sheldon, Larick, &amp; Carawan,&#xA0;2002), and outcomes from breaking facilities and unsellable eggs (El&#x2010;Deek, Al&#x2010;Harthi, &amp; Attia,&#xA0;2011). Eggshells are waste products that are generated by different sources such as home, hatcheries, and fast food industry and which causes environmental pollution. The disposal challenges include eggshells include cost, availability of disposal places, smell, flies, and abrasiveness. However, they can be utilized for plants, human, and animal nutrition and produce collagen (Amu, Fajobi, &amp; Oke, 2005).
        Eggshell has been reported possessing many important nutrients such a calcium and trace amounts of other micro&#x2010;elements, that is, magnesium, boron, copper, iron, manganese, molybdenum, sulfur, silicon, and zinc (King'ori, 2017). A medium&#x2010;sized complete eggshell could give 750&#x2013;800&#xA0;mg of calcium. The calcium with magnesium and vitamin D increases mineral bone density (Schaafsma et&#xA0;al.,&#xA0;2018). Eggshells are also used as a cheap source of calcium in fertilizing plants (Amu et al.,&#xA0;2005). Shell membrane is an important by&#x2010;product source for collagen which can be used medicinally and industrially (Ogawa, Portier, Moody, &amp; Bell, 2004). Also, eggshell and membrane contain many biologically active peptides (Table&#xA0;18; Nakano, Ikawa, &amp; Ozimek,&#xA0;2003).

        Meat

      4
      CONCLUSIONS
      To summarize, food processing by&#x2010;products accumulate in tons and cause huge environmental and economic problems in different stage and food industry sectors including fruits, vegetables, cereals, meat, dairy products, eggs coffee, and tea. If this would be studied and exploited carefully, they can be recycled and reused in different areas such as food industry, pharmaceuticals, and other biotechnical areas. These food by&#x2010;products are still containing many important biologically active compounds including fatty acids, amino acids, vitamins, minerals, dietary fibers, and antioxidants which can be useful economically and pharmaceutically (e.g., antimicrobials) in different aforementioned sectors instead of throwing into the ground. It is worth mentioning that different novel techniques have been examined in order to increase the potent of recovery of bioactive compounds from food by&#x2010;products. They are superior to conventional methods. This attributes the fact that they are eco&#x2010;friendlier, less hazardous, and less expensive. Therefore, it is important to choose a method that suits the intended bioactive compound to be extracted from different parts of the by&#x2010;product.
